Figure 4. Identification, interpretation, and stability assessment of the Minimal Interpretable Feature Set (MIFS).

(A) Exploratory feature-discovery workflow. The 46 engineered protein-level descriptors were ranked using an XGBoost model fitted to the complete dataset. Fixed subsets of top-ranked descriptors were subsequently evaluated by five-fold stratified cross-validation. The Top5 Logistic Regression model achieved a mean ROC-AUC of 0.673 and contained transporter-like annotation, mean inter-TMD gap, TMD negative-charge variability, TMD number, and ER localization. Removal of the manually curated transporter annotation yielded the four-feature MIFS, with a fixed-subset mean ROC-AUC of 0.672. Because feature discovery preceded fixed-subset cross-validation, these values are interpreted as exploratory benchmarks rather than fully selection-independent performance estimates.

(B) Standardized Logistic Regression coefficients from the MIFS model fitted to the complete protein-level dataset. Positive coefficients indicate positive statistical associations with WT-lost remodeling susceptibility, whereas negative coefficients indicate negative associations. Coefficients are used for direction-of-association interpretation and do not establish causal effects.

(C) Fold-wise assessment of feature-selection stability. XGBoost ranking was repeated using only the training partition of each of five stratified folds, transporter-like annotation was excluded when present, and the four highest-ranked remaining descriptors were evaluated on the held-out fold using Logistic Regression. Bars indicate the number of training folds in which each descriptor was selected. Blue bars identify descriptors belonging to the original full-data MIFS; gray bars identify alternative selected descriptors. The resulting compact models achieved a mean held-out ROC-AUC of 0.632 ± 0.054 and PR-AUC of 0.149 ± 0.062. Variation in the selected descriptors indicates partial rather than complete feature-selection stability.
